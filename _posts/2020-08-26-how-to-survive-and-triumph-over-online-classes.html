As a seasoned university online class taker of 2 months, let me share with you the 2 key strategies of winning at online school. It is super simple, like all things in life should be.

Follow (some sort of) schedule

Since lectures are pre-recorded, it’s easy to just throw them to the back of your head, and realize you have a heap of 15 lectures * 1 hour each = 15 hours of lectures to go through before that exam in 12 hours. To avoid this, I suggest creating a schedule of when you will listen to class.

It doesn’t even have to be your actual course schedule. If you’re not in the Vancouver timezone, maybe create a new schedule, and place each course in some time slot that you’d like. For example, let’s listen to three lectures of physics Monday 10-1, and then Math on Tuesday, and so on and so forth. As long as you actually follow the schedule, it gives you a lot of flexibility in how you’d like to schedule your learning. And once in a while, you could even give yourself a few days off in the middle of the week to meet up with friends, or just to relax, and do some extra overtime in the other days.

You can really schedule however you like, but the most important part is to stick to it. Which reminds me, I should go make mine!

Be organized

This is a very obvious one, but hard to do. At least when there were physical classes, you’d see friends and peers, and you’d hear all about that homework assignment due tomorrow, or that midterm coming up. Even if you didn’t keep track of these dates yourself, it was never really a big deal.

Now, pre-recorded lectures just don’t provide the same effect. Not only will we be in a different room than everyone else, we probably won’t even be listening to lectures at the same time. It’s more important now than ever to keep track of assignment deadlines, homeworks and exams.



You could possibly do this with an agenda if you’re old-school, or use a tool like Google Calendar if you like electronic copies. Throughout the term, I like to put important dates on Google Calendar, such as when a project is due or all the midterm exam dates. Then, I always know ahead of time if there’s something coming up that I should prepare for.

Those are the 2 things I think are the most important for online schooling. And to be completely honest, I probably didn’t do either of those well the first time around when we had online classes back in March. So I will be aiming for these 2 important points, and see if it improves my online class experience. Best of luck to everyone!
